Terminology Guide

ISRC (International Standard Recording Code)
A unique 12-character identifier for sound recordings and music videos. Format: CC-XXX-YY-NNNNN where CC=country, XXX=registrant, YY=year, NNNNN=designation.
Metadata Confidence Score
A percentage indicating the reliability of aggregated metadata based on source validation, cross-referencing, and data completeness.
Audio Features
Technical characteristics of a track including tempo (BPM), key, time signature, energy, danceability, and valence (musical positivity).
Credits
Attribution information including composers, lyricists, producers, performers, and engineers associated with a recording.
ISWC (International Standard Musical Work Code)
A unique identifier for musical works (compositions) as opposed to recordings. Links to publishing and composition rights.
UPC (Universal Product Code)
A barcode used for tracking trade items, often associated with physical or digital album releases.
Publishing Rights
Legal rights associated with the composition of a song, including mechanical, performance, and synchronization rights.
API Coverage
The percentage of tracks that have successfully retrieved metadata from a specific data source (Spotify, YouTube, etc.).
Bulk Analysis
Processing multiple ISRCs simultaneously to generate comprehensive metadata reports for entire catalogs.
Force Refresh
Bypasses cached data to retrieve the latest information directly from all connected APIs.
